Vrinda Store Data Analysis — 
Project Overview
This project analyzes Vrinda Store sales data to identify revenue trends, top-performing categories, customer segments, and operational opportunities. The workbook consolidates transactional sales records into an analysis-ready dataset and uses Excel-based exploration (Pivot Tables, formulas, charts, and dashboards) to uncover actionable business insights.
The goal is to transform raw retail data into decision-ready insights that support sales growth, inventory planning, and customer-focused marketing.
Problem Identified
Retail sales data often exists as thousands of rows of transactions, making it difficult to quickly answer questions such as:
1.	Which products and categories drive most revenue?
2.	Which months show peak or weak sales performance?
3.	Which customer segments contribute the highest sales?
4.	Where should inventory and promotional efforts be focused?
What I Did
1.	Imported and structured the Excel dataset for analysis.
2.	Cleaned and standardized fields (dates, categories, states/regions, customer attributes, order values).
3.	Created Pivot Tables and summary metrics for sales, orders, and revenue contribution.
4.	Built visualizations to compare trends across time, products, geography, and customer groups.
5.	Derived KPIs such as total sales, category share, top products, and monthly performance.
Key Insights
1.	Sales are concentrated in a small set of high-performing categories/products, indicating a strong Pareto effect.
2.	Revenue shows clear monthly seasonality, with distinct peak periods and slower months.
3.	Certain regions/states contribute disproportionately to overall sales, suggesting opportunities for targeted expansion.
4.	Customer demographics (for example, age group/gender where available) reveal segments with higher average order value or purchase frequency.
5.	A limited number of SKUs generate a large share of revenue, highlighting candidates for inventory prioritization and promotional focus.
KPIs Tracked
Metric	Purpose
Total Sales / Revenue	Overall business performance
Order Count	Transaction volume
Average Order Value (AOV)	Customer spend per order
Category Revenue Share	Product mix analysis
Monthly Sales Trend	Seasonality detection

Business Impact / Outcome
1.	Enables faster reporting from raw transactional data.
2.	Helps prioritize high-revenue products and categories.
3.	Supports inventory planning around seasonal demand patterns.
4.	Guides region- and segment-specific marketing decisions.
5.	Provides a reusable Excel analysis framework for future retail datasets.
Tech Stack
•	Microsoft Excel (Pivot Tables, formulas, charts, dashboarding)
•	Data cleaning and transformation performed within Excel
